Pre-Great War

Commonwealth Military Forces (1901-1914)

The Australian Military Forces (Army) was founded in 1901. Until 1914, the AMF was divided into two units. The first full-time unit was called the "Permanent Forces", and the part-time unit was called the "Civilian Forces".

Commonwealth Naval Forces (1901-1911)

The Australian Naval Forces were formed in 1901. The CNF was not as organized as the AMF.

The Great War

Australian Military Forces (1914-1921)

In 1914, an expeditionary unit was formed called the "Australian Imperial Force". The AIF also included the newly created Australian Flying Corps. In 1916, the CMF was renamed to the "Australian Military Forces".

Royal Australian Navy (1911-1921)

In 1911, the ANF received royal patronage, and from then on was called the "Royal Australian Navy".

Creation of the RAAF

Royal Australian Air Force (1921-1980)

In 1920, the AFC was renamed to the "Australian Air Corps". The next year it became a separate branch, the "Royal Australian Air Force" or RAAF.
